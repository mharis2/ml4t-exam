Welcome to Machine Learning for  Trading (CS7646)
This course is designed to give you the skills and knowledge needed to implement machine learning algorithms ef fectively in the trading sector . You'll learn to develop algorithmic trading strategies using advanced machine learning models. Throughout the course, we will cover a variety of
techniques in Supervised Learning—including linear regression, K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN), regression trees, and ensemble learners—and in Reinforcement Learning, focusing on Q-Learning. You'll apply these methods directly to real stock market data, making informed trading decisions.
But this course is about more than just coding. It takes a comprehensive approach to applied machine learning, incorporating lessons on machine learning theory , financial market analysis, trading strategy development, and academic research writing. Each element of the curriculum is designed to
build a thorough understanding of both the theoretical and practical applications of machine learning in trading, preparing you for future academic and professional challenges.
In the ML4T  course, students will develop a simulated trading system through iterative projects, culminating in a capstone project that integrates machine learning and algorithmic trading strategies to model the complexity of financial markets. For more details, visit the project overview page .
Get ready to dive deep into both the technological and financial aspects of trading as you develop the skills to innovate and navigate in this dynamic field!
Understanding Our  Course Policies
Our policies play a crucial role in setting the expectations and standards that will guide our interactions and your participation throughout the semester . These guidelines are designed to ensure a fair , respectful, and enriching learning environment for everyone involved. From communication and
submission protocols to academic integrity and professional conduct, each policy helps streamline our processes and provides clarity on what is expected of you as a student. We strongly encourage you to review these policies thoroughly to understand your responsibilities and the resources
available to you. This will not only help you navigate the course more ef fectively but also enhance your overall learning experience.
Please take a moment to familiarize yourself with the detailed course policies her e.
Exploring Our  Textbooks and Learning Resour ces
Our curriculum carefully integrates video content, required readings, and supplemental books to provide a comprehensive understanding of key concepts. The textbooks and other materials have been thoughtfully chosen to ensure you are engaging with the most insightful content available. While
we have specified certain essential readings, such as 'What Hedge Funds Really Do', which is a concise yet informative resource that can be comfortably read in an evening or two, not all materials require cover -to-cover reading. Additionally , all required and supplemental texts are freely
accessible online, ensuring you have ready access to these valuable educational tools.
For a detailed list of all the textbooks, including required and supplemental readings, please visit our Video Lessons, Textbooks, and Other  Resour ces page.

All the tools you need for this course are conveniently located online. Let's walk through where everything is and how to get there:

Remember , staying or ganized and keeping track of due dates is key to your success in this course. If you have any questions about the logistics or need help finding something, please reach out to the ML4T  Instructional Staf f on Ed Discussions.

Grades
Your performance in this class is assessed through a combination of dif ferent assignments, each designed to measure various aspects of your learning:
1. Projects : You will complete eight hands-on projects that form the backbone of your grade. These are your opportunities to apply what you've learned and demonstrate your problem-solving skills.
2. Exams : Two comprehensive exams will challenge your understanding of the course material and your ability to apply theoretical concepts.
3. Course Content Quizzes : Regular quizzes help reinforce your learning and give you immediate feedback on how well you're grasping the content.
4. Course Surveys : Four surveys will be conducted to gather your input on the course, which also contribute to your final grade.
Final grades are not just numbers; they reflect your hard work and progress. They will be calculated based on the weighted average of all graded components. Here's how the final letter grade breaks down:
90 or above secures an A
80 to 89 earns a B
70 to 79 gets you a C
60 to 69 results in a D
Below 60 leads to an F
Remember , the grade scale here is absolute, not curved. On the one hand, this means that everyone in class has the opportunity to aim high and achieve "A"s. On the other hand, it means that a score of 89.9999%, for example, is a B. Geor gia Tech does not use plus and minus grade.
Regarding the return of grades, our of ficial policy is to to release them in two main batches—one before the Institute's Drop Date, and the final batch at the end of the term. We understand the importance of timely feedback, so our hope is to be able to get grades back to you on a rolling basis,
Course Deliverables
Assignments: 71%
In this course, you will complete eight assignments, which together comprise 71% of your final grade. These assignments vary in scope and dif ficulty , and as such, they are weighted dif ferently to reflect their complexity and the ef fort required.
The assignments are designed as a mix of programming tasks and comprehensive projects that include both programming and written reports. This structure is intended to not only test your technical abilities but also to enhance your skills in analyzing, articulating, and presenting your findings in a
structured academic format. Each assignment will challenge you to apply the concepts and techniques discussed in readings and lectures to real-world trading scenarios, helping you to solidify your understanding and refine your skills in practical applications.

Exams: 25%
You’ll have two exams in this course, and each one will count for 12.5% of your overall grade.
The exams will be administered online using Canvas and Honorlock. You'll have a one-week window to complete each exam, starting one week before the due date. Make sure to schedule your exam within this period, as early or late submissions won’ t be accepted.
Everything we’ve covered up to the exam—including readings, any papers, YouTube videos, and Ed Lessons videos listed in the syllabus—is fair game for the exams. Be sure to review all these materials to prepare thoroughly and give yourself the best chance to excel.
Course Content Quizzes: 2%
Each week, you'll be greeted with a quiz designed to keep you sharp and engaged with the course material. During the Summer term, expect about 10 quizzes, while the Fall and Spring terms will feature around 12. These quizzes will touch on everything from the readings and lectures to the finer
points of your active projects.
Here's the deal with quizzes:
They'r e quick : You'll only have to tackle a few multiple-choice or multi-select questions.
They'r e convenient : You'll find them on Canvas, ready to go whenever you are within the week they're assigned.
They'r e a little differ ent fr om exams : While exams are all about multi-select questions, quizzes will mix it up with both multiple-choice and multi-select options.
They'r e uniquely scor ed: Canvas has its own way of grading quizzes, which is dif ferent from how we'll score your exams.
A heads-up on quiz etiquette: Please keep quiz content between you and Canvas. That means no sharing questions on Ed Discussion, Slack, Discord, or other chat platforms. It's part of ensuring everyone gets a fair shot at the material.
Quizzes open up each Monday and need to be completed by Sunday night at 1 1:59 pm AOE. It's a good rhythm—you start the week with new material and end with a quiz to cap it of f. Just remember , there are no early birds or latecomers in quiz land—everyone gets the same window to complete
them.
Course Surveys: 2%
Think of course surveys as your space to be heard — and they’re worth 2% of your overall grade. However , as there are no right or wrong answers, completing all four surveys is an easy way to earn 2% of your overall course grade.
